Target Vocabulary

You can edit this list and meanings right here before class.

  • mountain — a very high hill.
  • ocean — a very large area of salt water.
  • take a nap — sleep for a short time in the day.
  • floor — the ground inside a room; what you walk on.
  • heart attack — a serious medical emergency when the heart stops working well.
  • function — what something does; its purpose (verb: to work).

Practice • Pantomime Game

Pantomime (Charades) — 7–10 mins

Goal: students act out a word, classmates guess; fast recall + lots of laughs.
Setup: groups of 3–4; give each group a stack of word cards. One student draws a card and silently acts it.
Rules: no speaking, no writing, no pointing to letters; 45–60 seconds per turn.
Scoring (optional): +1 point per correct guess. Rotate actor every round.

Production • Free use

Production — 10–15 mins

Students use the new vocabulary in real communication with minimal teacher help.

Option A — Mini‑Dialogues: pairs create a 4–6 line dialogue using at least three words. Quick share-outs.
Option B — Story Sprint: groups write a 4–5 sentence story that naturally includes the words.
Option C — Role‑play: assign a situation (trip to the ocean, hiking a mountain, first aid for a heart attack). Students improvise.

Presentation • Set the task • CCQs

Presentation — 10–15 mins

Lead‑in: quick photo prompts (mountains, the ocean, someone yawning, etc.) — elicit ideas.
Model & Meaning: show word → mime or picture → chorally drill pronunciation → write form.
CCQs: simple yes/no or either/or checks (e.g., “Is a mountain bigger than a hill?”).
Transition: explain the upcoming game and demo one round with a volunteer.
